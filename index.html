Project Overview

Accurate estimation of cellular age is critical for understanding developmental biology and age-related pathologies. However, traditional machine learning models struggle with the high dimensionality and sparsity inherent in single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) data. In this work, we introduce scLLM-Age, a novel framework that adapts foundational Large Language Models to interpret transcriptomic profiles. By treating gene expression sequences as biological "tokens," our approach enables zero-shot and few-shot age prediction across diverse cell types.


Contributions

  • 1) A novel tokenization strategy that efficiently maps continuous scRNA-seq expression values into a discrete embedding space suitable for transformer-based LLMs.
  • 2) State-of-the-art performance in age prediction on human and murine single-cell datasets, outperforming traditional regression and random forest models.
  • 3) The release of a curated, standardized benchmark dataset containing over 500,000 annotated single cells spanning 15 distinct age time-points.
